Frequently Asked Questions

Got questions? We've got answers! Here's everything you need to know about Lunaris Hacks.

Registration will open soon! Keep an eye on our social media and website for announcements. When registration opens, you'll be able to sign up through our registration portal. We'll also send out notifications through our mailing list.

Registration deadlines will be announced when registration opens. We recommend registering early as spots may be limited. Follow our Instagram @lunarishacks for the latest updates on important dates.

Lunaris Hacks is open to all types of engineers and engineering students:

  • Currently enrolled university or college students in any engineering program
  • Recent graduates (within 2 years)
  • All engineering disciplines welcome: mechanical, biomedical, computer, civil, electrical, and more
  • Both individual participants and teams are welcome

Bring:

  • Your laptop and chargers
  • Student ID for verification
  • Comfortable clothes and any personal items
  • Your creativity and enthusiasm!

We'll provide:

  • Workspace and power outlets
  • WiFi and basic development tools access
  • Food and beverages throughout the event
  • Hardware and components for projects (limited quantities)

Food: We'll provide meals, snacks, and beverages throughout the event. We'll accommodate dietary restrictions - just let us know during registration.

Accommodation: This is primarily a day event, but we'll have designated rest areas available. Specific accommodation details will be shared closer to the event date.

We're working on exciting prizes for various categories including innovation, impact, and technical excellence. Prize details and distribution information will be announced closer to the event. Winners will be selected by a panel of industry experts and mentors.

Project submissions will include:

  • A working prototype or demonstration
  • Project presentation (5-10 minutes)
  • Brief technical documentation
  • Code repository (if applicable)

Detailed submission guidelines will be provided to participants during the event.

You retain full ownership of your intellectual property and any projects you create during the hackathon. Lunaris Hacks and sponsors do not claim any rights to your work. We only ask for permission to showcase winning projects for promotional purposes.

Registration for Lunaris Hacks is free! If you need to cancel your registration, please let us know as soon as possible so we can offer your spot to someone on the waitlist. Cancellation policies for any paid workshops or premium tiers will be clearly outlined during registration.

Basic technical requirements:

  • Laptop with your preferred development environment
  • Access to coding tools and software you're comfortable with
  • No specific software requirements - use what works best for your project
  • We'll provide internet access and basic hardware components

Detailed technical specifications will be shared with registered participants.
